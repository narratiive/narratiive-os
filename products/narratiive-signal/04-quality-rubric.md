# Narratiive Signal — Quality Rubric

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

## Scoring

Score each dimension from 1–10. Total possible score: 100.

A Signal may proceed to human review only when:

- total score is at least 90;
- no dimension scores below 8;
- all mandatory gates pass.

## Dimensions

### 1. Specificity

Could the observation only have been written for this business?

### 2. Strategic consequence

Does the observation reveal a meaningful growth, brand or competitive opportunity rather than a cosmetic communications point?

### 3. Evidence integrity

Are material claims grounded, proportionate and traceable? Are assumptions labelled?

### 4. Originality

Does the Signal give the recipient language or perspective they are unlikely to receive from a generic agency audit?

### 5. Coherence

Does one clear argument connect the observation, evidence, white space and creative direction?

### 6. Editorial quality

Is the writing warm, concise, human and free of corporate or AI-generated texture?

### 7. Creative fertility

Does the strategic idea naturally produce a compelling campaign world rather than a bolted-on execution?

### 8. Brand plausibility

Could the idea credibly belong to this brand while still moving it somewhere new?

### 9. Judgement

Does `What we'd be careful about` identify a genuine tension and demonstrate mature strategic thinking?

### 10. Commercial invitation

Does the work create desire for a conversation without becoming a sales pitch?

## Mandatory gates

The Signal automatically fails if any answer is no:

- Is there one central insight?
- Is every material factual claim supported?
- Are assumptions clearly identified?
- Is the observation genuinely specific to the company?
- Does the creative reinforce the insight?
- Is the external document free of internal notes and placeholders?
- Is the invitation low pressure?
- Would Narratiive be comfortable sending this to the company's founder or CEO?

## Revision protocol

When a Signal fails:

1. Name the exact deficient dimension or gate.
2. Preserve the evidence record and raw previous version.
3. Return targeted revision instructions to Claude.
4. Re-score the complete revised output rather than only the edited section.
5. Record the score change and revision rationale.

Do not inflate scores to move work through the pipeline.
