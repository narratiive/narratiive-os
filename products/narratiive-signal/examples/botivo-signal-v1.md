# Narratiive Signal — Botivo Drinks Worked Example

Status: Reference example

This example records the structure and strategic spine of the Botivo Signal discussed during product development. It is a reference for quality and form, not copy to be reused for another company.

## Personal opening

We spent some time looking at Botivo because it sits in a particularly interesting place: not simply as a non-alcoholic alternative, but as a brand capable of creating a ritual and meaning of its own.

## What we noticed

Botivo's opportunity is larger than replacing alcohol. The strongest future for the brand may be to own a distinctive moment of the day — a grown-up pause that feels chosen rather than compromised.

## Why we think that

The premium non-alcoholic category is often framed through absence: no alcohol, fewer consequences, an alternative to the established serve. Botivo's product, craft story and visual character give it permission to compete through presence instead — flavour, ritual, atmosphere and intention.

## The white space

Aperol has culturally encoded the bright, social early evening. Botivo could build a different but equally recognisable occasion: a more personal golden hour, defined by the drinker rather than the venue or the crowd.

## If this were ours...

We would explore **Your Own Golden Hour**.

Not another aperitivo campaign, but a platform that turns the transition between the demands of the day and the evening ahead into a Botivo ritual. Golden hour becomes emotional rather than merely photographic: the moment someone consciously returns to themselves.

## What we'd be careful about

The work should not become worthy, wellness-coded or anti-alcohol. Botivo is still a pleasurable, complex adult drink. The opportunity is to add meaning without removing appetite, sociability or sensuality.

## A speculative campaign world

Quietly cinematic scenes of people claiming an early-evening moment in very different ways: the last light through a kitchen window, a studio being closed for the day, friends arriving before dinner, a solitary glass outdoors after work. The liquid, serve and ritual are tactile and central. The tone is warm, confident and intimate rather than euphoric or preachy.

Suggested asset pair:

1. A short film moving between several personal versions of golden hour, united by the preparation and first sip of Botivo.
2. A still image built around low evening light, tactile ingredients and the line `Your Own Golden Hour`.

## Why this example works

- It expresses one central opportunity.
- The idea is specific to Botivo's category position and product character.
- The strategic observation naturally creates a campaign world.
- The caution demonstrates judgement rather than undermining the idea.
- The close invites a response without forcing one.
