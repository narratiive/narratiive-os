# Narratiive Signal — Review Checklist

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical
Audience: Tony and human approver

## Intake

- [ ] Company is approved for outreach.
- [ ] Recipient and role are correct.
- [ ] Signal number is unique and sequential.
- [ ] Public-source permissions and any private inputs are recorded.
- [ ] Latest canonical files were loaded before generation.

## Research and reasoning

- [ ] Sources are relevant, current enough and traceable.
- [ ] Facts, interpretations and assumptions are separated.
- [ ] The central observation is specific to this business.
- [ ] The observation has commercial or brand consequence.
- [ ] Rejected alternatives and selection rationale are retained internally.

## Document

- [ ] Personal opening explains genuine interest in the business.
- [ ] `What we noticed` contains one clear observation.
- [ ] `Why we think that` uses sufficient evidence without becoming a research dump.
- [ ] `The white space` names an ownable opportunity.
- [ ] `If this were ours...` offers one memorable organising idea.
- [ ] `What we'd be careful about` identifies a real risk or trade-off.
- [ ] The campaign world makes the idea tangible.
- [ ] The invitation is warm and low pressure.
- [ ] The final document is concise and contains no internal scaffolding.

## Creative

- [ ] Every asset reinforces the central insight.
- [ ] The visual world feels plausible for the brand but shows a new future.
- [ ] Product and branding behaviour are clear.
- [ ] Prompts specify exclusions and likely failure modes.
- [ ] Creative demonstrates taste rather than spectacle for its own sake.
- [ ] The recipient should be able to imagine approving or producing the idea.

## Outreach email

- [ ] Subject line is natural and not clickbait.
- [ ] Opening is warm and precisely personalised.
- [ ] Email is 100–180 words unless there is a strong reason otherwise.
- [ ] It does not repeat the entire Signal.
- [ ] It does not use fake urgency or an aggressive meeting request.
- [ ] Attachment names and links are correct.

## Quality and release

- [ ] Total rubric score is 90 or higher.
- [ ] No dimension is below 8.
- [ ] All mandatory gates pass.
- [ ] Claude has revised any failed dimensions.
- [ ] Tony has stored raw output, sources, scores and version lineage.
- [ ] Human approval has been recorded.
- [ ] Final Gmail draft is addressed to the correct recipient.

## Final human test

Before sending, answer yes to all five:

1. Would the recipient feel understood?
2. Is there a sentence they are likely to remember?
3. Does the creative make the strategic point more desirable?
4. Does the work feel worth keeping?
5. Would I be proud to send this to the founder personally?

If any answer is no, return the Signal for revision.
