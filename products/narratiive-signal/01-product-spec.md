# Narratiive Signal — Product Specification

Version: 1.1
Status: Canonical

## Definition

A Narratiive Signal is an unsolicited strategic gift for a carefully selected business. It gives language to an opportunity the recipient may already feel but has not yet clearly articulated.

It is not a free strategy deck, an audit, a proposal, a generic agency credential or a disguised meeting request.

The recipient-facing Signal is a concise strategic dispatch. It is not the complete internal research, workflow record or creative production pack.

## Purpose

A successful Signal makes the recipient feel:

- understood rather than targeted;
- intrigued rather than sold to;
- challenged without being patronised;
- able to picture a more distinctive future for the brand;
- motivated to continue the conversation.

The primary editorial objective is maximum curiosity, not maximum information.

The intended response is: `If this is what they noticed from the outside, I would like to know what they would find working with us.`

## Required inputs

- Company and brand name
- Website and relevant public channels
- Named recipient and role where available
- Public evidence about the business, category, customers and communications
- Reason the company belongs on the Narratiive outreach list
- Any known commercial context or constraints

Assumptions must be identified. Unsupported certainty is prohibited.

## Required output package

The complete working package contains separate deliverables. They must never be collapsed into one recipient-facing document.

1. Internal work record and evidence lineage
2. Short personalised outreach email
3. Recipient-facing Narratiive Signal
4. Creative production brief for:
   - two short moving-image concepts; or
   - one moving-image concept and one still concept
5. Final quality score and review checklist

Only items 2 and 3 are automatically recipient-facing. Approved creative previews may be embedded in the Signal, but production prompts, source notes, rejected ideas, scoring and workflow commentary must remain internal.

## External identity

Header format:

`NARRATIIVE SIGNAL`

`No. [sequential number]`

`For: [Company]`

The sequential number must be genuine. Narratiive should only produce a limited number of Signals each month.

## Canonical recipient-facing structure

### 1. Personal opening

One warm sentence explaining why Narratiive spent time with this particular business. It should feel naturally written to the recipient, not mail-merged.

### 2. What we noticed

One specific, consequential observation about the business, brand, category or customer tension. It must not be generic enough to apply to ten competitors.

### 3. Why we think that

Use no more than three concise evidence points. Include only the evidence needed to make the observation credible. This is not a research dump.

### 4. The white space

Name the underused opportunity in direct language. Explain what the company could own, express or make culturally meaningful that competitors currently do not.

### 5. If this were ours...

Present one strategic and creative direction with a memorable organising idea. Make the possibility tangible without turning the Signal into a full campaign plan.

### 6. What we'd be careful about

Name the principal risk, tension or trade-off in one concise passage. This demonstrates judgement without opening a second strategic argument.

### 7. Invitation

End with a low-pressure invitation. The preferred posture is: `If this sparks a conversation, we'd enjoy comparing notes.` Do not use an aggressive meeting request.

## Creative preview rule

The Signal may contain one dominant visual or up to two concise speculative creative frames. Their purpose is to help the recipient picture the opportunity, not to demonstrate production volume.

Detailed treatments, alternative routes and Higgsfield prompts belong in the internal creative production brief, not in the Signal.

## Hard length and design rules

### Outreach email

- 100–150 words preferred; 180 words absolute maximum.
- Must fit within one normal laptop viewport without scrolling.
- Must not summarise every section of the Signal.

### Recipient-facing Signal

- 500–800 words preferred; 900 words absolute maximum excluding short source footnotes.
- One to two designed pages preferred; three pages only when a visual genuinely requires space.
- Under three minutes to read.
- One central insight, one white-space opportunity and one creative direction.
- No internal workflow, catalogue, prompt, scoring or production notes.

If the work exceeds the maximum length, the generating model must edit it down before presentation. It must not justify extra length by citing the amount of research completed.

## Success criteria

The Signal succeeds when:

- the founder or decision-maker feels accurately understood;
- the central insight is memorable and genuinely specific;
- the evidence is sufficient and trustworthy;
- the creative possibility is desirable and easy to picture;
- the document feels worth keeping;
- the recipient can grasp the argument quickly;
- the next conversation feels like the recipient's choice.

A reply is a useful commercial outcome, but it is not the only definition of quality.
