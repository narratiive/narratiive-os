# Narratiive Signal — Product Specification

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical

## Definition

A Narratiive Signal is an unsolicited strategic gift for a carefully selected business. It gives language to an opportunity the recipient may already feel but has not yet clearly articulated.

It is not a free strategy deck, an audit, a proposal, a generic agency credential or a disguised meeting request.

## Purpose

A successful Signal makes the recipient feel:

- understood rather than targeted;
- intrigued rather than sold to;
- challenged without being patronised;
- able to picture a more distinctive future for the brand;
- motivated to continue the conversation.

## Required inputs

- Company and brand name
- Website and relevant public channels
- Named recipient and role where available
- Public evidence about the business, category, customers and communications
- Reason the company belongs on the Narratiive outreach list
- Any known commercial context or constraints

Assumptions must be identified. Unsupported certainty is prohibited.

## Required output package

1. A short personalised outreach email
2. One Narratiive Signal document
3. A speculative campaign world expressed through either:
   - two short moving-image concepts; or
   - one moving-image concept and one still concept
4. Source notes and evidence lineage for internal use
5. A completed quality score and review checklist

## External identity

Header format:

`NARRATIIVE SIGNAL`

`No. [sequential number]`

`For: [Company]`

The sequential number must be genuine. Narratiive should only produce a limited number of Signals each month.

## Canonical narrative structure

### 1. Personal opening

A warm sentence explaining why Narratiive spent time with this particular business. It should feel naturally written to the recipient, not mail-merged.

### 2. What we noticed

One specific, consequential observation about the business, brand, category or customer tension. It must not be generic enough to apply to ten competitors.

### 3. Why we think that

A concise explanation grounded in public evidence. Use only the evidence needed to make the point credible. This is not a research dump.

### 4. The white space

Name the underused opportunity. Explain what the company could own, express or make culturally meaningful that competitors currently do not.

### 5. If this were ours...

Present one strategic and creative direction. Give it a memorable organising idea or campaign platform. Show how it connects the observation, customer truth and commercial opportunity.

### 6. What we'd be careful about

Demonstrate judgement by naming the principal risk, tension or trade-off. This section should make the work more credible, not weaken it.

### 7. A speculative campaign world

Describe the visual and emotional world that makes the idea tangible. The creative assets should follow this direction and feel plausible for the brand while showing a future it has not yet occupied.

### 8. Invitation

End with a low-pressure invitation. The preferred posture is: `If this sparks a conversation, we'd enjoy comparing notes.` Do not use an aggressive meeting request.

## Length

The Signal should normally fit within 2–4 designed pages and take under five minutes to read. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Success criteria

The Signal succeeds when:

- the founder or decision-maker feels accurately understood;
- the central insight is memorable and genuinely specific;
- the evidence is sufficient and trustworthy;
- the creative possibility is desirable;
- the document feels worth keeping;
- the next conversation feels like the recipient's choice.

A reply is a useful commercial outcome, but it is not the only definition of quality.
