# NARRATIIVE BLUEPRINT SCHEMA v3

## V3.1 UPDATE (RAVE REVIEW LEARNINGS)

- Slide 27 revision: "So What" must describe the business implication of the creative direction.
- Slide 27 must not reference selling the next phase.
- The required structure is Creative Territory -> Business Outcome -> Strategic Role.
- Slide 28 revision: rename to "Growth Priorities and Dependencies".
- Slide 28 may extend beyond marketing.
- Separate marketing-controlled actions, cross-functional dependencies and business-level constraints.
- Slide 29 revision: remove prioritisation logic.
- Slide 29 is a pure Measurement and Learning Framework.
- Slide 29 must cover Leading Indicators, Performance Indicators, Brand Indicators, Review Cadence and Decision Rules.
- Audience Upgrade Rule: demand pools are discovered, not categorised.
- Prefer behavioural definitions such as "Supermarket-Plus Switcher", "Weekend Ritualist" or "Subscription Sceptic".
- Target quality threshold: 9.5/10 founder-grade.

## PURPOSE

This schema defines the fixed 30-slide structure for the Narratiive Growth Blueprint.

It is the canonical structure for all blueprint population work.

## SOURCE OF TRUTH

The structure below is the canonical blueprint architecture.

It is not a suggestion.

It is the fixed frame that Blueprint population must fill.

## CANONICAL STRUCTURE

The blueprint contains 30 slides across 6 acts.

### Act 1 — The Case for Change

### Slide 1 — Cover

- Purpose: Establish the client, title and strategic product.
- Visual Type: Title lockup.
- Layout Type: A.
- Content Requirements:
  - Client name
  - Blueprint title
  - Version or date
- Inputs:
  - Client identity
  - Blueprint metadata
- Outputs:
  - Canonical cover slide
- So What Test: Does this feel like a serious strategic product?

### Slide 2 — Executive Thesis

- Purpose: State the core strategic argument.
- Visual Type: Founder statement.
- Layout Type: A.
- Content Requirements:
  - Thesis
  - Strategic consequence
  - Confidence level
- Inputs:
  - Evidence pack
  - Founder insight
- Outputs:
  - One-sentence thesis
- So What Test: Can the founder repeat the argument in one line?

### Slide 3 — The Commercial Question

- Purpose: Define the business question the blueprint must answer.

- So What Test: Is the question commercially meaningful?

## POPULATION RULES

- Keep the 30-slide structure intact.
- Maintain the six-act architecture.
- Do not add new slides.
- Do not collapse slides together.
- Keep the founder-grade language direct and specific.
- Make the business consequence of each slide explicit.

## DERIVATIVE OUTPUT MAPS

- Executive summary must derive from Slides 1-5.
- Competitive diagnosis must derive from Slides 4-10.
- Audience summary must derive from Slides 11-15.
- Positioning summary must derive from Slides 16-22.
- Growth system summary must derive from Slides 23-26.
- Implementation summary must derive from Slides 27-30.
